Function block library

Clear building blocks your team can understand.

The call flow is not hidden in code. Each function has a clear job, which makes the system easier to review, maintain, test and improve as your business changes.

Function type What it does Example use Maintainer value
Input Collects information from the caller
Captures names, phone numbers, email addresses, service type, urgency, booking requests and free-text answers.		 “What service do you need?” / “What area are you in?” Easy to see exactly what data is being gathered and where it is saved.
Voice & AI Speaks, listens and interprets intent
Uses controlled prompts and AI conversation blocks to understand what the caller wants.		 Qualify a new enquiry or classify a missed call message. Prompts can be reviewed and improved without rebuilding the whole system.
Decision Routes the call based on rules
Checks answers, business hours, caller type, urgency, booking intent or saved record data.		 Urgent job goes to staff; non-urgent enquiry goes to callback flow. Decision paths are visible, testable and easier to troubleshoot.
Actions Does the follow-up work
Sends SMS, creates records, books appointments, triggers callbacks, sends emails or hands off to a person.		 Send confirmation SMS after booking or notify the team after a new lead. Every automation action can be traced back to a specific block.
System Controls reliability and flow behaviour
Handles delays, retries, linked flows, webhooks, API lookups and fallback routes.		 If no answer, retry once, then send SMS and create a callback task. Safer to maintain because fallback behaviour is designed, not guessed.
Integrations Connects to calendars, CRM and dashboards
Reads or writes business data so the AI worker fits the existing operation.		 Check staff calendars, save a lead, or update a customer record. Shows exactly where external systems are involved.
This is the key difference: the business does not just get an AI receptionist. It gets a visible, maintainable call-handling system that can be reviewed, changed and improved over time.
