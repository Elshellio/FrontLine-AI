Terms of Service

Plain-English terms for Frontline AI services.

These terms explain how Frontline AI projects, support, delivery, warranty and service limits are intended to work. They are written for clarity, not as heavy legal drafting.

This is a client-facing placeholder terms page. It should be professionally reviewed before being relied on as legal advice or used as final contractual wording.

1. The service

Frontline AI designs and builds controlled AI worker, website, automation and knowledge-assistant workflows for businesses. The exact scope, deliverables, content, integrations, review steps and support arrangements should be confirmed in writing before work starts.

Where a project depends on third-party tools, hosting, APIs, email, SMS, booking systems or other external services, those services remain subject to their own terms and availability.

2. Client responsibilities

Clients are responsible for providing accurate information, approved copy, access where needed, brand assets, workflow decisions, review feedback and any legal or compliance approvals required for their own business.

Frontline AI can recommend sensible controlled delivery steps, but the client remains responsible for confirming that the final workflow is suitable for their business, industry and customers.

3. 12-month code warranty

Frontline AI provides a 12-month code warranty for Frontline AI code and workmanship that we created as part of the agreed project.

The warranty is intended to cover defects caused by our own code or implementation work. If a covered defect is reported within the warranty period, Frontline AI will review the issue and, where it is confirmed as a covered defect, take reasonable steps to fix it.

Warranty remediation may include repairing code, adjusting configuration we supplied, restoring intended behaviour, or proposing a controlled workaround where that is the most practical route.

4. What the warranty covers

  • Defects caused by Frontline AI code we created.
  • Workmanship issues in agreed deliverables.
  • Behaviour that does not match the agreed tested scope because of our implementation.
  • Reasonable investigation of reported defects during the warranty period.

5. What the warranty does not cover

  • Third-party service outages.
  • Hosting or provider failures outside our control.
  • API changes by third parties.
  • Client edits or changes made by others.
  • New feature requests or expanded scope.
  • Misuse or unsupported configuration changes.
  • Issues caused by unpaid or unauthorised modifications.

6. Reporting a problem

To report a suspected defect, provide a clear description, the page or workflow affected, screenshots or recordings where useful, the expected result, the actual result, and the approximate time the issue occurred.

Frontline AI will triage the issue, confirm whether it appears to be covered by the warranty, and explain the next practical remediation step. Urgent issues affecting live enquiries or bookings should be flagged clearly when reported.

7. Change requests and new work

Changes outside the agreed scope, new features, new integrations, new content structures, redesigns, workflow expansions and improvements based on later business decisions are handled as separate change requests or ongoing support work.

Frontline AI uses controlled change, testing and review so live business workflows can be improved without avoidable disruption.

8. Limits and sensible expectations

No digital service can guarantee uninterrupted performance, fixed commercial results or permanent compatibility with every third-party service. Frontline AI aims to build practical, controlled systems that can be reviewed, tested and improved over time.

Where a service includes AI-assisted workflows, the agreed build should define what the system is allowed to answer, collect, route or escalate.
